## COVER LETTER BEST PRACTICES

### Before Sending:

1. **Research the company thoroughly** — Read their blog, try their product, understand their values
2. **Find the hiring manager's name** — LinkedIn, company website, or "Hiring Team" if unavailable
3. **Customize every letter** — Generic letters are obvious and ineffective
4. **Keep it to one page** — Maximum 400-500 words
5. **Proofread meticulously** — Typos in a cover letter for a writing job is fatal

### What to Emphasize:

- **Specific company knowledge** — Shows genuine interest, not mass application
- **Relevant portfolio pieces** — Link directly to work that matches their needs
- **Understanding of their audience** — Developers? Researchers? Enterprise customers?
- **Value proposition** — Not just what you want, but what you'll give them

### What to Avoid:

- Generic openings ("I'm excited to apply...")
- Listing skills without context
- Making it about what you'll learn (make it about what you'll contribute)
- Overly long letters (respect their time)
- Negativity about current/past employers
